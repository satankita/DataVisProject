Gross Nation Income (GNI) Males vs. Females

Although the income ratio between males and females do not have a direct correlation to the economic development status of a country, generally, most of the developed countries have a ratio around 1.5 or less. There are exceptions to this, where some developed countries have a ratio less than 1.5, mostly due to cultural reasons. While most of the developing countries have a ratio higher than 2, there are exceptions with some developing countries having a ratio around 1.2, but this is more indicative of the factors contributing to weaker economic conditions.
